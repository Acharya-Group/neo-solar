Solar Panels

Solar Services ‚Äì Complete Details

Solar panels are working by converting sunlight into electricity through a process called the photovoltaic effect. Each panel contains many solar cells made from silicon. When sunlight (photons) hits these cells, electrons get energized and start moving, generating an electric current. The produced electricity is DC (Direct Current), which cannot be used directly by most home appliances.

Types of Solar Systems

üî∑ 1. On-Grid Solar System (Grid-Tied System)
  • Connected directly to the electricity grid.
  • Extra solar power is sent to the grid using Net Metering.
  • No battery required, so installation cost is low.
  • Works only when grid supply is available.
  • Best for homes, shops, factories with stable power supply.
üî∑ 2. Off-Grid Solar System (Stand-Alone System)
  • Works independently without grid connection.
  • Uses batteries to store extra power.
  • No Net Metering available.
  • Ideal for remote areas or frequent power cut locations.
  • Higher cost due to battery requirement.
üî∑ 3. Hybrid Solar System (On-Grid + Off-Grid Combination)
  • Combination of on-grid and off-grid systems.
  • Stores extra power in batteries + supports Net Metering.
  • Works even during power cuts.
  • Best for areas with power cuts but wanting bill savings.
  • Slightly higher cost but most flexible solution.

How Solar Panels Work

DC power generated from panels is sent to a solar inverter where it is converted into AC (Alternating Current) ‚Äî the electricity used in homes and industries. The generated power can be used instantly, stored in batteries, or supplied back to the grid.
